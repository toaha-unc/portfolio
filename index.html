About Me

My Journey

Hi Im Toaha Siddique. I'm a full stack software developer and recent graduate of FastTrack. My tech stack is Java, Spring Boot, Git, PostgreSQL, JavaScript and Angular. I was born in Bangladesh, became a US citizen, and lived in both countries. Now I've settled in the US full time. I was introduced to programming in high school, and I loved and enjoyed programming a lot. I loved solving complex programming problems, and after graduating from high school, I majored in Computer Science at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill. I was able to continue my journey in programming and computer science. So, I did an internship at the Computer Science Experience labs. There, I worked on a project where we created a seat and room reservation system for students. We worked in a team. We did this project to make it easier for the students to book seats and reserve rooms, so that they could collaborate and work on projects with each other. After graduating from college, I pursued a career as a professor. I taught software engineering. I became a Professor because I remember my time in high school when I was introduced to programming I didn't have any good instructor. So I taught software engineering. So, I learned a lot throughout my whole journey of programming and software engineering. I learned that my goal was to help people who are less fortunate, and my goal was to solve complex problems. I enjoyed doing it!

I specialize in modern web technologies and enjoy working on projects that challenge me to think creatively and push the boundaries of what's possible on the web.
